About Me

I'm originally from Winnipeg, Manitoba, and graduated from Kelvin High School in 2014. Currently, I am studying in Montreal at McGill University in the Honours Economics program as a Ralph M. Barford Loran Scholar.

Academically, my interests focus on data accessibility and data-driven development. In the summer of 2015, I worked for the International Institute for Sustainable Development (IISD) on a community indicator system called Peg. This summer, I am in Tasajera, El Salvador developing a small-scale community indicator system with Emana El Salvador. The website for this project will be up and running soon. Be sure to check it out!

Since beginning my studies at McGill, I have been involved in many student groups and extra-curricular activities. In first year, I was VP External of the Douglas Hall Council and sat on the McGill Inter-Residence Council. I play for the McGill Ultimate Team and sing baritone in an a cappella group called Effusion. This past year, I worked as a Floor Fellow in Douglas Hall, supporting first-year students in residence.

For more information about me, check out my LinkedIn page or send me an email at franklinkirkwright@gmail.com.
