Though I am originally from Winnipeg, I now live in Montreal and study in the Honours Economics program as a Loran Scholar at McGill University with minors in Computer Science and International Development Studies. I am fluent in English, French and Spanish.

In the summer of 2015, I worked for the Intenational Institute for Sustainable Development on Peg, a community indicator system serving Winnipeg. The following summer, I teamed up with Daniel Teodoro to develop Mareas de Tasajera, a low-cost community indicator system in San Rafael la Tasajera, El Salvador. We built the framework for the project in early 2016 and spent that summer in El Salvador implementing the pilot edition. We presented the results at the Community Indicators Consortium's annual Impact Summit in the fall, and the project continues to grow.

This summer, I am working at Scotiabank in Toronto as an intern in the Academic Partnerships department.
